The Queen from the Lewis Chess

The queen from the Lewis chess set is possibly the most famous piece in the collection, for her bored expression. In contrast to the rooks, figured as semi-mythological berserkers, the queen is unexpectedly ordinary in her pose. When found, some of the Lewis chess men were stained red, so this is not necessarily the white queen. In this 360 view, the characteristic Norse decoration of the back of the piece is clearly visible. Perhaps this was ornate precisely because one of the two players would spend most of the game with this view of his own piece.
